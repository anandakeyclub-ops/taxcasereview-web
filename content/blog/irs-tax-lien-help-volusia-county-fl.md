## How Federal Tax Liens Work in Florida

The lien process starts when you receive a Notice and Demand for Payment. If you don't pay within 10 days, the IRS assesses the liability and files a Notice of Federal Tax Lien. In Volusia County, this filing happens at the Clerk's office in Daytona Beach, making it visible to credit bureaus, potential employers, and anyone who searches public records. Ignoring a tax lien in Volusia County won't make it disappear—it empowers the IRS to seize your assets through levies.

## Your Resolution Options

**Installment Agreement** — This is the most common solution for resolving an IRS tax lien in Volusia County. You make monthly payments until the debt is satisfied. The IRS offers various plans depending on how much you owe. If you owe $50,000 or less, you can often set up a streamlined agreement online. While the lien remains in place during your payment plan, you stop collection activity and prevent levies.

**Offer in Compromise** — This program allows you to settle your tax debt for less than you owe if you meet strict financial criteria. The IRS examines your income, expenses, and asset equity to determine what you can reasonably pay. Many taxpayers don't qualify, but for those who do, it's the fastest way to eliminate debt. A successful offer can resolve years of tax problems for pennies on the dollar.

**Penalty Abatement** — The IRS charges penalties for late filing and late payment, which can increase your debt by 25% or more. If you have reasonable cause—like a medical emergency, natural disaster, or reliance on bad advice from a tax professional—you may qualify to have penalties removed. This won't eliminate the underlying tax, but it significantly reduces what you owe and stops penalties from accruing.

**Lien Withdrawal** — Different from a lien release, a withdrawal removes the public Notice of Federal Tax Lien as if it never existed. You might qualify if you enter a direct debit installment agreement, if the lien was filed prematurely, or if withdrawal helps with tax collection. This option protects your credit rating and makes selling property in Volusia County much easier.

**Currently Not Collectible Status** — If you genuinely cannot pay your basic living expenses and your tax debt, the IRS may temporarily stop collection efforts. You'll still owe the money, and interest continues to accrue, but the IRS won't levy your bank account or garnish wages. This status gives you breathing room to get back on your feet financially.
