Once filed at the El Paso County Clerk's office in downtown El Paso, this lien becomes public record and attaches to everything you own—your home, your car, your business assets, and even property you acquire in the future. ## How Federal Tax Liens Work in Texas

The IRS doesn't file a lien without warning. You'll first receive a Notice and Demand for Payment, then a Final Notice of Intent to Levy and Notice of Your Right to a Hearing (CP90 or LT11). You have 30 days from that final notice to respond before the IRS files the lien with El Paso County. Once filed, the lien appears on your credit report and can drop your score by 100 points or more. In Texas, where many residents work in healthcare, military-related positions at Fort Bliss, or run small businesses in the service industry, a damaged credit score affects everything from security clearances to vendor relationships. The IRS also sends a copy of the lien filing to you, but by then the damage is done—it's already public. If you continue ignoring the problem, the IRS moves to the next step: levying your bank accounts, garnishing wages, or seizing property. Time matters more than most El Paso County taxpayers realize.

## Your Resolution Options

**Installment Agreement**: This is a monthly payment plan that lets you pay off your tax debt over time. The IRS will work with you if you owe less than $50,000 and can prove you need time to pay. Once you're in an active installment agreement and making payments, the IRS may subordinate or withdraw the lien under certain conditions, which can help your credit and ability to sell property.

**Offer in Compromise**: This program allows you to settle your tax debt for less than you owe, but only if you meet strict financial criteria. You must prove you cannot pay the full amount before the collection statute expires. The IRS accepts less than 40% of offers submitted, so documentation and preparation matter significantly. Results vary. Every situation is unique.

**Penalty Abatement**: If you have a clean payment history or experienced a genuine hardship—medical emergency, natural disaster, or serious financial setback—you can request that the IRS remove penalties. This won't eliminate the tax you owe, but it can reduce your total debt by 25% or more in some cases.

**Lien Withdrawal**: Different from a lien release, a withdrawal removes the public Notice of Federal Tax Lien as if it was never filed. You might qualify if you're in a Direct Debit Installment Agreement, owe $25,000 or less, and meet other requirements. This option specifically helps repair credit damage.

**Currently Not Collectible Status**: If you're facing genuine financial hardship and cannot pay anything right now, the IRS may temporarily stop collection efforts. Your debt doesn't disappear, and interest continues accruing, but this status gives you breathing room during unemployment, medical crisis, or other hardship.
